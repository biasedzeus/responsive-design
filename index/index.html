Robert Falcon Scott

The Expedition of his of life time
Robert Falcon Scott's last image admitting defeat at the South Pole.
  • 15th June 1910 -Terra Nova sails from Cardiff, Wales
  • 4th Jan 1911 -Arrival at Cape Evans, McMurdo Sound, Antarctica. Ship unloaded and hut built on Ross Island.
  • 25th Jan 1911-Terra Nova leaves Cape Evans, she will spend the winter in New Zealand
  • 27th June 1911 -Bowers, Cherry-Garrard, Wilson make a winter journey to Cape Crozier to bring back an emperor penguins' egg in the Antarctic winter night
  • 2nd Aug 1911Cape Crozier party return exhausted after a difficult journey
  • 24th Oct - 4th Nov 1911South Pole journey begins, 16 men, 2 motor sledges, 10 ponies, 23 dogs, 13 sledges set off
  • 24th Nov 1911-First 2 men sent back to Cape Evans
  • 9th Dec 1911-Foot of Beardmore Glacier reached, ponies shot
  • 11th Dec 1911-Next 2 men and dog team sent back
  • 21st Dec 1911-Half way up Beardmore Glacier, next 4 men sent back
  • 4th Jan 1912-Rim of Beardmore, 3 men sent back, 5 now head to the South Pole
  • 17th Jan 1912-5 men reach the South Pole to find they were beaten by Amundsen a month earlier
  • 6th Feb 1912-Terra Nova arrives at Cape Evans
  • 17th Feb 1912-Edgar Evans dies in his sleeping bag on the return from the pol
  • 3rd Mar 1912-Terra Nova leaves Cape Evans after waiting for the South Pole Party to return, she cannot risk being iced in as winter arrives
  • 16th Mar 1912-Suffering from gangrene and frostbite, Captain Oates walks to his death in a blizzard sacrificing himself for his companions
  • 19th Mar 1912-Scott's final camp made 11 miles from food and fuel at One Ton Depot, the three surviving men can walk no further
  • 29th Mar 1912-Scott dies in the tent, he was presumed to be the last man alive
  • 12th Nov 1912 - The bodies of Scott, Bowers and Wilson are found in their tent
  • 18th Jan 1913-Terra Nova arrives at Cape Evans
